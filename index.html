Primary Sources

Explore authentic documents and materials written by or about key figures like Omar ibn Said, Nicholas Said, Job (Ayyub) the son of Solomon, and Yarrow Mamout.

Omar Ibn Said. The Autobiography of Omar Ibn Said: A Bilingual Edition.

This bilingual edition presents the autobiography of Omar Ibn Said, an enslaved African Muslim scholar in the United States. Written in Arabic, it is a unique narrative that blends personal experience with Islamic teachings. The work offers insights into Omar's intellectual and spiritual resilience despite the harsh realities of slavery. The bilingual format enhances accessibility and provides a deeper understanding of Omar’s original text and its cultural context.

Bilingual Edition of Omar Ibn Said

Said, Nicholas. The Autobiography of Nicholas Said.

This autobiography narrates the extraordinary life of Nicholas Said, a freed African slave who became a world traveler and educator. Said chronicles his early life in West Africa, his enslavement, and eventual emancipation, providing a rare perspective on the experiences of African Muslims in the 19th century. The text explores themes of identity, perseverance, and adaptation, offering a remarkable look at cross-cultural encounters.

Nicholas Said Memoir

Bluett, Thomas. Some Memoirs of the Life of Job (Ayyub ibn Suleiman Diallo).

Bluett recounts the story of Ayyub ibn Suleiman Diallo, a Muslim scholar from West Africa who was enslaved in the Americas and later freed. The narrative provides a detailed account of Job’s journey, from his capture to his return to Africa, highlighting his unwavering Islamic faith and intellectual prowess. This memoir is a vital historical document shedding light on the intersection of slavery and religion in the 18th century.
